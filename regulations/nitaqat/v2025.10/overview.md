# Nitaqat (Saudization) Overview

## Introduction

The Nitaqat program is Saudi Arabia's localization initiative aimed at increasing employment opportunities for Saudi nationals in the private sector.

## Article 1: Program Objectives

The main objectives of the Nitaqat program are:
- Increase employment of Saudi nationals in private sector companies
- Reduce unemployment among Saudi citizens
- Develop the skills and capabilities of the Saudi workforce

## Article 2: Classification System

Companies are classified into four bands based on their Saudization percentage:

### Red Band

Companies in the red band have Saudization rates below the minimum threshold for their sector and size. They face restrictions on government services and work visa issuance.

### Yellow Band

The yellow band represents companies approaching the minimum requirements. While not facing immediate restrictions, these companies are encouraged to improve their Saudization rates.

### Green Band

Companies in the green band meet or exceed the minimum Saudization requirements. They are eligible for government services and can apply for new work visas.

### Platinum Band

The platinum band recognizes companies with exceptional Saudization rates. These companies receive priority in government services and may qualify for additional incentives.

## Article 3: Sector-Specific Requirements

Different economic sectors have varying Saudization requirements based on industry characteristics and labor market dynamics.

### Retail Sector

The retail sector has relatively higher Saudization requirements due to the availability of Saudi talent and the nature of retail work.

### Construction Sector

Construction sector requirements are lower, recognizing the technical nature and physical demands of construction work.

### Information Technology

The IT sector has moderate requirements with emphasis on training and development programs for Saudi nationals.

## Article 4: Company Size Bands

Saudization requirements vary by company size:

- **Small (1-9 employees):** Lower requirements to support small business growth
- **Medium (10-49 employees):** Moderate requirements with flexibility
- **Large (50-499 employees):** Standard requirements applicable
- **Very Large (500-2999 employees):** Higher expectations for large employers
- **Enterprise (3000+ employees):** Highest requirements with comprehensive compliance

## Article 5: Monitoring and Compliance

The Ministry of Human Resources and Social Development (HRSD) monitors Nitaqat compliance through:

- Monthly data updates from GOSI (General Organization for Social Insurance)
- Integration with Mudad and Qiwa platforms
- Real-time calculation of Saudization percentages
- Automated notifications to employers

## Article 6: Penalties and Incentives

**Penalties for Non-Compliance:**
- Restrictions on new work visa issuance
- Limitations on government services access
- Inability to renew existing work visas
- Administrative fines for repeated violations

**Incentives for Compliance:**
- Priority processing of government transactions
- Access to HRDF training subsidies
- Recruitment support services
- Recognition programs for top performers

## Article 7: Exemptions and Special Cases

Certain positions and circumstances may qualify for exemptions:

- Highly specialized technical roles with no Saudi talent available
- Executive positions requiring specific international experience
- Temporary project-based work
- Positions in newly established companies (grace period)

## Article 8: Improvement Strategies

Companies can improve their Nitaqat status through:

- Hiring qualified Saudi nationals
- Participating in training and development programs
- Partnering with educational institutions
- Offering competitive salaries and benefits to Saudi employees
- Creating career development pathways

## Article 9: Reporting Requirements

Employers must:

- Register all employees in GOSI within 15 days of hiring
- Maintain accurate employee records
- Report any changes in employment status
- Monitor their Nitaqat status monthly via Qiwa portal
- Respond to HRSD inquiries promptly

## Article 10: Support Services

The government provides various support services:

- Jadarat (national recruitment platform)
- HRDF training and development programs
- Qiwa platform for compliance management
- Business advisory services
- Partnerships with private sector recruitment agencies
